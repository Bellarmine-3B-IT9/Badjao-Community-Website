DONASYON SAGA BADJAO


Our project was designed to help and sustain the Badjao Community in its struggle with financial troubles. Due to the institution's
expanding membership and the rise in the cost of essentials, financial difficulties have been a major problem. The institution doesn't have enough funds to support all of its members, and they are struggling to pay for food, high-quality healthcare, education, and other
expenses like water and electricity bills. The institutions' beneficiaries and benefactors, particularly those affected by the pandemic,
struggled to find alternative sources of income because of the lockdown, which stopped people's jobs and livelihoods. The institution is
struggling and needs more funding to fully support all of its members now that it has more than 500 members. Our project's main
objective is to assist the institution in raising money so that it can fully support each of its members. The project will increase awareness
and encourage people to donate money and assist those in need. The project will target individuals of all ages with the sincere goal of
assisting our Badjao friends in having a better quality of life. Since we are only organizing a fund-raising campaign project, our budget will
only be 8,000 pesos. Since we will be using the internet to fund raise, we won't need money for anything other than the onsite
conversation with the institution after the project is implemented. All of the money we raise through the campaign will be given to the
institution and its members. With the project, the institution will have the opportunity to develop a higher standard of education and a health program for its members,
particularly in light of the occurrence of viruses and disease today. In addition, the members will receive money to spend on food, better housing, clothing, and other
necessities. Nevertheless, by being able to assist those in need, the project can also benefit those who contributed to the fund raising.

Profile

Mission

For its members, the badjaos, the Badjao Community Institution works to promote various livelihood programs. No matter their age, we frequently see Badjaos on the street working and begging for alms; even young children from the tribe must work. This is why the organization made sure to develop programs that greatly assist the Badjoas, like family planning and health care, literacy initiatives, and making sure they have access to decent healthcare and education. They also make sure to engage in environmental preservation, and they have training facilities for the preservation of the Badjaos cultural heritage.

History

The association started with the guidance of Pastor Felicisimo “Jun” Morales. Pastor Jun, together with his wife, lived with the Badjao in Isla Verde for more than 8 years. ​The association was established with the purpose of becoming a model among the populace of the badjao community and was made due to the Bajao Community’s pressing issues about basic needs and common problems experienced that gave an impact on the community. According to the article members of the Badjao Community lost business and capital, which lead to selling their pre-loved items that the member of community-owned just to buy food, Medicine such as paracetamol, purgative, and bandage for the wound.

Beneficiaries

The Badjaos, an ethnic group found close to the shores of Mindanao, Indonesia, and other islands, are the people who are being admitted to the institutions. The Badjaos are also referred to as "Sea Gypsies." The majority of Badjaos are Muslims, and they originally thrived by trading and fishing in addition to being well-known for their beautifully woven sails. However, in search of jobs and a decent standard of living, the Badjoas have now scattered across the country.
