Compost

The composting process requires composts. A combination of materials called compost is used to nourish plants and enhance the biological, chemical, and physical properties of soil to encourage more productive and robust plant growth. Fruit and vegetable scraps can be composted along with other organic waste to enhance soil health, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, recycle nutrients, and lessen the impact of droughts. Due to its ability to bind soil together to slow down and infiltrate water, compost can be used to assist prevent soil erosion. In addition to giving the soil with necessary nutrients, compost also boosts the soil's ability to hold nutrients. It improves nutrient retention and soil structure by increasing the cation exchange capacity (CEC) of the soil then provides the plants with the nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
